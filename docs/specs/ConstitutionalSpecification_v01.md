# Sovereign Reconstruction Engine (SRE) — Constitutional Specification v0.1

## Preamble

The Sovereign Reconstruction Engine (SRE) is governed by the CIEMS Sovereignty Stack.
All components operate under constitutional constraints, evidence validation, and governance oversight.

## Article I — Constitutional Invariants

1. No constitutional decision without constitutional evidence.
2. All evidence must pass FAC-E1 → FAC-E4 validation.
3. All reconstructions must pass FAC-V1 → FAC-V5 validation.
4. All AI outputs must be evidence-constrained and governance-traceable.
5. All services must operate under Sovereign Certificate authority.

## Article II — Evidence Layer Specification

### Section 1 — EvidenceRegistry

Implements FAC-E1 Authenticity, FAC-E2 Integrity, FAC-E3 Provenance, FAC-E4 Constitutional Fit; integrates with Dantomax.

### Section 2 — Evidence Model

Immutable, hash-chained, provenance-tracked, constitutionally tagged.

## Article III — FRA Reconstruction Specification

### Section 1 — Nine-Stage FRA Cycle

OBSERVE → EXTRACT → BUILD → TEST → REFINE → ALIGN → VALIDATE → CERTIFY → ARCHIVE

### Section 2 — Reconstruction Constraints

Evidence-constrained iteration, drift detection, progressive refinement, constitutional validation.

## Article IV — AI Layer Specification

### Section 1 — HLRMAIAgent

Pattern extraction, proto-form hypothesis generation, FRA-guided refinement, constitutional validation.

### Section 2 — AI Constraints

No unconstrained generation; all outputs evidence-anchored and governance-traceable.

## Article V — Governance Layer Specification

### Section 1 — CIH Service

Project approval, evidence baseline, architecture review, HYFAL Council hearing, Sovereign certificate issuance.

### Section 2 — Governance Trace

Every reconstruction run must produce a constitutional trace; every decision must be reconstructable.

## Article VI — Enterprise Layer Specification

Multi-environment configuration, security & compliance, monitoring & logging, microservice deployment.

MCRL temporal mapping; cross-period reconstruction alignment.
